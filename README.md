## Flight Techniques & Cinematic Skills

- **Reveal Shot** – Fly upward or sideways to gradually reveal landscape details.
- **Orbit (Point of Interest)** – Circle around a subject for cinematic dynamism.
- **Dolly-In/Dolly-Out** – Move forward/backward while adjusting gimbal tilt.
- **Parallax Shots** – Foreground elements move faster than background.
- **Top-Down (“Nadir”) Shots** – Iconic mapping-style overhead imagery.
- **Hyperlapse / Timelapse Aerials** – Smooth long-time-motion sequences.

## Aerial Photography Techniques

- **Golden Hour & Blue Hour Timing** – Best natural lighting.
- **HDR Exposure Bracketing** – Balanced exposure in complex scenes.
- **Panorama Modes**
  - Sphere
  - 180°
  - Vertical
  - Wide
- **Focus Peaking & Manual Focus** – For cinema drones with adjustable lenses.
- **RAW Shooting** – Maximizes post-production flexibility.

## Cinematic Shooting Modes

- **D-Log / D-Log M / HLG** – Flat profiles for professional color grading.
- **Cine Mode** – Smooth, slow, cinematic movement.
- **ActiveTrack** – Subject tracking with cinematic parameters.
- **Waypoint Missions** – Automated repeatable flight paths.
- **Cruise Control** – Smooth long-distance flight paths.
